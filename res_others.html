Modeling composability in embedded system through hybrid automata

For my BS thesis, I used the Hybrid Automata theory to model and simulate the feature of composability in an embedded system. Composability is a system design principle that concerns the inter-relationships of components and the additional properties that this may cause. The work was within the Artemis pShield, a FP7 European Project whose target was to address Security, Privacy and Dependability (SPD) in the context of Embedded Systems as built-in rather than as add-on functionalities. For the thesis, I built a vertical controller (the Overlay) in a multi-layer architecture and simulated its behavior through Simulink-Stateflow.

Semi-autonomous navigation of a humanoid robot

For one of my MS classes, I developed an algorithm of obstacle avoidance for a NAO humanoid consisting of repulsive force fields from NAO's ultrasounds. Together with my teammates, we validated the algorithm both in simulation (ROS + Webots) and on the real robot.
